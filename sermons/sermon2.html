Jesus : Time of Favour

Evang, S.K Pelemo| Luke 1:30-33 | 28 May 2023

Today, we delve into the remarkable truth that there is a perfect time set by God to pour out His blessings upon us. So, grab a cup of coffee, settle in, and let's discover the incredible ways God orchestrates His favor.

In the symphony of life, God conducts each moment with precision. Just as Psalm 102:13 reminds us, there is a designated time for God's favor to manifest in our lives. This realization fills our hearts with hope and anticipation, knowing that our heavenly Father has a remarkable plan for each of us. Throughout history, we encounter inspiring accounts that exemplify the power of God's favor. Joseph, despite enduring hardships, was suddenly called from the darkness of prison to serve as a prominent leader. Similarly, Mordecai, a humble servant, experienced a divine shift in circumstances when his appointed time of favor arrived. These narratives remind us that God's timing is never late; it is always perfect.

Notes

  • There is a time that has been set by God to show favour to humans. Psalm 102:13.

    • Thou shalt arise, and have mercy upon Zion: for the time to favour her, yea, the set time, is come.

  • On the day of his favour, Joseph was called from prison to serve as the prime minister.

  • Mordecai was a slave at the kings gate until his time of favour came.

  • Anything the devil can destroy in your life is not needed in your next level.

  • When your time of favour comes, even enemies begin to work for you..

  • That God blessed you does not stop the blessing of another person so rejoice when you see God’s blessings in someone else’s life, it means you’re next.

  • The blessings/grace of Jesus is enough to go round. Jesus left the feast to heal the man by the Bethesda pool because his time for favour had come.

  • Only the person himself, not demons or the devil himself can stop God when the set time of favour comes
