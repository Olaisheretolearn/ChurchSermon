Get Undistracted

Rev, Y.V Pelemo |1 Sam 1:1-15 | 14 May 2023

Drawing inspiration from 1 Samuel 1:1-15 and 1 Samuel 30, we shed light on the pervasive nature of distractions and offer insights on how to conquer them to emerge victorious.

In our modern world, distractions lurk at every corner, threatening to hinder our spiritual progress. They manifest in various forms, such as the allure of social media, domestic conflicts, the temptation of pornography, anger, exposure of children to illicit behaviors, excessive focus on work, the trap of offenses, the blame game, excuses, and the allure of material wealth.

Notes

  • We are on a spiritual battlefield. Eliminating distractions will help us fight the battle to win.

    • 11 And she made a vow, saying, “Lord Almighty, if you will only look on your servant’s misery and remember me, and not forget your servant but give her a son, then I will give him to the Lord for all the days of his life, and no razor will ever be used on his head.”.

  • In our daily lives, distractions abound: social media, domestic conflicts, pornography, anger, exposure to illicit behaviors, phone usage, work demands, offenses, the blame game, excuses, pity-parties, and the lure of money.

  • Hannah was not distracted by the words of Peninnah. Her situation also didn't keep her away from Shiloh.

  • We shouldn't leave the family unattended to pursue money. The family is the most important estate and we must not be distracted by money pursuit.

  • It is important to put parental controls for our children.

  • Being undistracted is what will make us call on God even in distress.

  • The devil has taken the war to our home. The war we are facing today is the fight for the family.

  • We must avoid the ephemeral things to stay undistracted.

  • We need the wisdom of God in running the home. The Word of God and prayer help make us wiser. The Word and prayer are key in fighting the battle.

  • We must seek the mind of Lord concerning situations in our home.
